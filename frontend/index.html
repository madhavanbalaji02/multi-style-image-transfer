üí° Key Findings

‚úÖ

LoRA Fine-tuning Superior

Van Gogh LoRA achieved 94% style accuracy, significantly outperforming both GAN (68%) and base Diffusion (82%) models. The fine-tuned adapter captures authentic brushstrokes and color palettes.

‚ö°

Speed vs Quality Tradeoff

GAN models are 10x faster (2.3s vs 26.8s) but sacrifice style fidelity. For real-time applications, GAN is preferred; for high-quality outputs, LoRA excels.

üé®

Style-Specific Performance

Pop Art and Van Gogh styles achieved highest CLIP scores (0.88, 0.92), while Renaissance struggled (0.76). Bold, distinctive styles transfer more reliably than subtle classical techniques.

üìä

Low-Rank Adaptation Efficient

LoRA adapter is only 6.1 MB (vs 3.4 GB full model), making deployment feasible. Training converged in just 10 epochs with stable gradient norms.

üî¨

LPIPS vs CLIP Insights

Lower LPIPS (0.21-0.23) indicates better perceptual similarity. Higher CLIP scores (0.89-0.92) confirm semantic style alignment. Both metrics validate LoRA's superiority.

Content Preservation Challenge

Diffusion models sometimes over-stylize, losing content details. GAN maintains content better but with less artistic fidelity. Balancing this remains an open challenge.
